What you need


  • a deck of cards
  • a flat surface to play on
  • someone to play against

Objective


The objective is to get the highest score possible without going over 21

The numbered cards are worth the points on the card

The royals(Jack,Queen,King) are worth 10

The ace is worth either 1 or 11 points depending on what the player wants


How to play

This assumes you are playing at home


the dealing

PLayers place their bets before the dealing starts

at the begining of the round the players and dealer are dealt two cards

The player's cards are normally dealt face up

The dealer has one card dealt face-down(this is known as the hole) and one face-up

The best possible inital hand to be dealt is an ace and one card worth 10 points

This is known as blackjack or 'natural 21'

if aplayer gets a blackjack they automatically win unless the dealer also has a blackjack in which case it is a tie

If the dealer has a blackjack any players without a blackjack automatically lose

The player's turn


The player first say if he is going to use any of the side rules(explained later)

Then the player can keep his hand (stand) or take a card from the deck (hit)

The player can hit until he decides to stand or exceeds 21 and loses (goes bust)

The dealer's turn


Once all the players have finished their actions the dealer turns over his hidden card

If the dealer has a blackjack then all the palyers lose (unless they also have a blckjack)

If the dealer doesn't have a blackjack then they either hit or stand

However the dealer hits and stands based on rules:

if he has less than 17 he must hit and if has more he must stand

When at home change the dealer after every hand to keep it fair or have someone whois the appointed daler the whole night

The side rules

Insurance

When a dealer's face-up card is an ace the players have the choice to bet on wether the dealer has a blackjack or not

The insurance bet is equivalent to the bet placed and is payed back at odds of 2:1 if the dealer does have a blackjack whilst the origninal bet will be lost. so the player basically breaks even

surrender

If the player has a bad hand in comparison to the dealer(based on what they can see) they can give up their hand and reclaim half of their bet

Splitting

If a player gets two starting cards of the same score then he can split into two hands

the player puts another bet on the new hand that is the same size as the bet on the original hand

the dealer will then deal a second card to each of the new cards

The player then treats the two hands seperately so can go bust or stand and then do the same on the second hand

Double down

If th player is confident their the hand will beat the dearler's hand you may double your original bet

Betting


At the end of each round one of four things will happen for the player

  • Lose (where the player's bet is taken by the dealer
  • Win(The player wins as much as he bets and keeps his own money,doubling how much they have)
  • BlackJack (The player wins 1.5 times their bet and keeps his own money)
  • Tie (The player gets back the money they bet)
