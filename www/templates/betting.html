At the start

Players in poker act in turn


In clockwise rotation


when it is a player's turn the first verbal declaration of action binds them to that action


until the first bet is placed, players have two options:


check


open

Check

This is the equivalent of betting zero


This can only be done when no one else has bet


It shows the player doesn't want to open but still wants to hold their cards to bet/raise/call later.

Open

If in any betting round it is the player's turn to act and the action is unopened the player can open action in a beting round by making a bet


this is added to the pot to increase it's size


n no-limit and pot-limit games, there is a minimum amount that is required to be bet in order to open the action

After Open

after someone has opened the option to check is not allowed the players now have three options


call


raise


fold

Call

To call a is to match a bet or match a raise


a betting rounds ends when all players have bet and equal amountor when everyone folds to another player's bet or raise


if no players call another player's rise then that player wins the pot

raise

To raise is to increase the size of an existing bet in the same betting round


Standard poker rules require that raises must be at least equal to the amount of the previous bet or raise.

for example


if an opponent bets £5 then the player must raise by at leat £5(they cannot rise by £2)


If the player raises by £7(bringing it up to £12)


if next player decides to raise then they have to raise by at least £7 pounds(taking the pot to £19)

When playing fixed-limit games of poker the size of bets and stakes is specified


For example in $3/$6 fix limit hold'em the first two betting rounds all opens and raises must be $3


after the river all opens ad raises must be $6

fold

To fold is to discard one's hand and forefit intrest in pot


No more bets are required from the player but they also can't win
